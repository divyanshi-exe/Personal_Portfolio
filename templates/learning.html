Group Discussion
Group Discussion (GD) is a structured form of discussion used to evaluate participants on communication skills, teamwork, leadership, critical thinking, and awareness of current issues. GDs can be of various types such as topic-based (factual, abstract, or controversial), case-based, or task-oriented. To perform well, one should be well-informed, confident, and respectful during the discussion. Do's in a GD include staying relevant, listening actively, speaking clearly, and encouraging others, while don’ts include interrupting, dominating the discussion, or going off-topic. Generating ideas in a GD can be easier if you break the topic into parts (social, economic, political), use examples, and apply frameworks like SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) or PESTLE (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental) for structured thinking.
