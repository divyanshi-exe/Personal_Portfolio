Movie Review: Dead Poets Society (1989)

Directed by: Peter Weir

Starring: Robin Williams, Robert Sean Leonard, Ethan Hawke

Watch Trailer

1) Your Favourite Dialogue and Why?

"Carpe Diem. Seize the day, boys. Make your lives extraordinary."

This quote stands out because it's the central message of the movie. It encourages students—and all of us—to live fully, make bold choices, and not be confined by societal expectations. It reminds me that time is fleeting, and we should make the most of every opportunity.

2) Your Personal Review

Dead Poets Society is a beautifully crafted film that explores the clash between conformity and individuality in a strict boarding school. Robin Williams shines as Mr. Keating, an English teacher who inspires his students to think for themselves and embrace poetry, creativity, and self-expression. The emotional depth, strong performances, and poetic storytelling make it a timeless classic. The film made me reflect on the importance of passion in education and the courage it takes to stand up for your beliefs.

3) Learning to Take Away from the Movie

  • Value of Individuality: Always think independently and don’t just follow the crowd.
  • Importance of Passionate Teaching: Great teachers can change lives by inspiring curiosity and confidence.
  • Living Authentically: It’s important to pursue what truly matters to us, not just what others expect.
  • Voice Matters: Everyone deserves to be heard and express their own thoughts and feelings.

4) Related Concepts You've Learned in the Classroom

  • Communication and Self-Expression: Just as we learn about expressing ideas clearly, the movie emphasizes the power of words and poetry.
  • Critical Thinking: Mr. Keating encourages the boys to analyze literature deeply and form their own interpretations—similar to what we’re taught in language and humanities classes.
  • Moral Courage: The film connects to ethical discussions in subjects like Social Studies or Value Education, where standing up for what's right is a key theme.
  • Leadership and Influence: Mr. Keating’s leadership style shows how influence can be positive and transformative, tying into discussions from psychology or leadership studies.
